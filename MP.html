Mechanical Properties

Stress and Strain

Consider a bar of original cross sectional area A
being subjected to equal and opposite forces F
pulling at the ends so the bar is under tension.
The material is experiencing a stress defined to 
be the ratio of the force to the cross sectional 
area of the bar, as well as an axial elongation:

        Stress = F/A
        Strain = L-L0/L0

Tensile Strength

- Maximum stress on Engineering stress-strain 
curve.
- Metals : occurs when noticeable necking starts.
- Polymers : occurs when polymer backbone chains 
are aligned and about to break.

Hardness

- Resistance to permanently indenting the
surface.
- Large hardness means : 
    - resistance to plastic deformation or cracking
    in compression.
    - better wear propertics.
